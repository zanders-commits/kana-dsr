Section 02  /  Business Case & Proposed Solution

The Problem You're Solving — and How Reeco Closes It.

Three pain points your team raised on June 4. Three direct answers.

01
The Problem

Manual Invoice Coding at Every Property

Your GMs and department heads code each invoice by hand, then batch scan to the AP team for entry into M3. Across 65 properties receiving 50 to 150 invoices each month, that is hundreds of hours of labor that produces no output other than a completed entry.

The Answer

AI Coding That Learns Your Team

Reeco routes every invoice — emailed in, scanned, or uploaded — through an AI engine that learns how each property codes its vendors. What takes 7 to 10 minutes per invoice is reduced to approximately 20 seconds. The first few weeks, the team codes manually so the system can learn; after that, invoices arrive in M3 pre-coded.

02
The Problem

No Visibility Until It Hits M3

Once an invoice leaves the property and reaches the AP team, there is no real-time status visible at the property level. GMs do not know what has been posted, what is pending, or what has been paid — until they log into M3 separately.

The Answer

Property-Level Visibility, Inside Reeco

Every invoice's status — received, coded, posted, paid — is visible from the Reeco dashboard without requiring a separate M3 login. GMs see exactly where each invoice stands, and approval gates can be set so nothing posts to M3 without the right person reviewing it first.

03
The Problem

Paper Invoices Still Arriving at the Front Desk

Not every vendor sends digital invoices. Paper invoices still land at the front desk, get handed to a GM, and enter a manual process with no tracking.

The Answer

Two-Tap Mobile Scanning

The Reeco mobile app lets any team member scan a paper invoice in two taps. The OCR engine processes it the same way as a digital invoice — it enters the AP workflow automatically, gets coded, and routes to M3 like any other invoice.

How the First Quarter Works

The first billing term is structured to give you a full evaluation period at minimal cost.

Pilot (5 properties): One month free. You pay for two of the three months in the first quarter.
Rollout (60 properties): Fully credited for all three months. No charge during the evaluation period.
Out clause: Written notice to Reeco 14 days before the end of the evaluation period closes the contract. You only owe what has accrued on the pilot properties. The rollout never activates.
Setup fee credit: The $2,500 pilot setup fee is credited back against your second-quarter invoice if you proceed with the full portfolio.
